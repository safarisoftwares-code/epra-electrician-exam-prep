📋 EPRA Exam Regulations Reference

All questions in this platform are based on the following official regulations and standards. Understanding these documents is essential for passing your EPRA certification exam.

Primary Standard

BS 7671:2018+A2:2022 - IET Wiring Regulations

The primary standard for electrical installations in Kenya. Covers all aspects of design, installation, inspection, and testing of electrical systems.

Part 4: Protection Part 5: Selection & Erection Part 6: Inspection & Testing Part 7: Special Locations Appendix 4: Cable Calculations
Kenya Specific

Kenya Power Distribution Code

Governs the distribution of electricity by Kenya Power. Specifies voltage levels, connection requirements, and supply standards for all consumers.

Supply Voltage: 240V/415V Frequency: 50Hz Earthing: TN-C-S (PME) Connection Policy
Kenya Specific

Energy Act 2019

The legal framework for all energy-related activities in Kenya. Establishes EPRA as the regulatory authority and sets licensing requirements for electricians.

EPRA Licensing Electrician Classes C, B, A Installation Certification Compliance & Enforcement
Kenya Specific

EPRA Electrical Installation Regulations

Specific regulations governing electrical installation work in Kenya. Covers licensing, certification, inspection requirements, and safety standards.

Completion Certificate Periodic Inspection Licensed Electricians Notification Requirements
Supporting Standard

Building Regulations Part M (Accessibility)

Specifies requirements for accessible electrical installations in buildings, including mounting heights for switches and socket-outlets.

Socket Height: 450mm min Switch Height: 1350mm Consumer Unit Access
Safety Guidance

Electrical Safety First - Best Practice Guide

Industry-recognized guidance on electrical safety, including EICR coding classifications (C1, C2, C3, FI) and safe isolation procedures.

EICR Codes Safe Isolation Periodic Inspection
Safety Guidance

HSE Guidance Note GS38

Health and Safety Executive guidance on electrical test equipment for use by electricians. Covers voltage indicators, proving units, and safe testing procedures.

Voltage Indicators Proving Units Test Equipment Safety
Supporting Standard

Code of Practice for In-Service Inspection & Testing

Guidance on PAT testing frequencies and procedures for portable appliances in commercial and industrial environments.

PAT Testing Inspection Frequencies Equipment Classes
